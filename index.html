Cats, Always

Quiet companionship, subtle affection, and that “chosen closeness” that makes a cat’s love feel extra special.

I’ve loved cats for as long as I can remember. Since childhood, I’ve always had at least two cats—so most of my earliest attachments come from cuddling them, caring for them, and learning how comforting their presence can be.

Part of My Whole Life

Cats have been a constant in my life from the beginning. They’re woven into my routines and memories—feeding them, playing with them, and feeling that steady calm that comes from sharing a space with a gentle creature.

My Childhood Companions

When my sister stayed home to babysit me, she was usually busy with schoolwork. My cats kept me company and made the house feel safe. Even on quiet days, they were there—like living comfort.

Why People Appreciate Cats

  • Quiet companionship: they’re present without demanding constant attention.
  • Subtle affection: slow blinks, head bumps, and cozy “I choose you” cuddles.
  • Unique personalities: every cat has their own tiny rituals and quirks.
  • Comfort in hard moments: their calm presence can feel grounding and steady.

Chosen Closeness

Cats are independent, but when they come close on their own terms, it feels meaningful. Their softness, calm beauty, and purring turn everyday life into something warmer.
